Duende Forest

1. The Rules of Their World

  • Never whistle at night. It calls them, and they do not always come in peace.
  • Do not disturb their homes. Hollow trees, caves, and abandoned places belong to them.
  • Always ask permission. Before entering the forest, before taking what is not yours, before speaking of them too loudly.

Eyewitness accounts and historical records confirm that those who break these rules often face consequences: illness, lost time, objects moving on their own, or far worse.

2. Small Feet in the Dirt: Unexplained Physical Evidence

  • Tiny footprints appearing overnight, leading nowhere.
  • Unusual claw-like scratches on walls and furniture.
  • Half-eaten food left in places where no one had been.

These physical traces suggest that duendes are not mere spirits but tangible beings that interact with the physical world when they choose to.

3. The Vanishing Children Phenomenon

  • A sudden change in personality — withdrawn, quiet, or oddly amused.
  • Speaking of “little friends” that no one else can see.
  • Waking up in strange places, unable to recall how they got there.

Some cultures leave offerings to the duendes to ensure a child's safe return. Others never speak of them at all, hoping silence will grant protection.
