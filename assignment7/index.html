They Walk Among Us

Duendes are small, mythical creatures found in the folklore of various cultures, particularly in Spain and Latin America. They are often described as mischievous beings, sometimes helping humans but other times causing trouble by stealing objects, making noises in the night, or leading travelers astray. Duendes falls into what can be called a "fictitious truth" a narrative that, while not verifiably real, holds cultural significance and remains compelling across generations.

The concept of a fictitious truth is fascinating because it illustrates how myths and legends serve a deeper purpose beyond mere storytelling. They convey cultural values, explain the unexplainable, and provide comfort or warnings. Whether or not duendes exist in reality, their presence in folklore suggests an enduring need to personify the mysterious and unpredictable aspects of life. To me, this is intriguing because it highlights how people across different cultures create similar mythological beings to make sense of their experiences. Duendes embodies the dual nature of tricksters both helpful and deceitful, reinforcing the idea that life itself is unpredictable and nuanced.
